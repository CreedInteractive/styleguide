Based on the grid concepts in the Bootstrap framework the styleguide comes with a 12 column fluid grid that can easily be changed to account for any amount of columns and gutter width. By default each column is 100% width and can be adjusted with each breakpoint (sm, md, lg, xl) to keep the content legible with an appropriate flow. Breakpoints are defined in ems instead of pixels.

In most cases it won’t make sense to have 12 columns on a small screen so in the example below each column starts off at 100% width by default (12 of 12 columns), then when more room becomes available each column takes up less width (6 of 12 then 3 of 12 then 2 of 12…) until each is 1 of 12 columns at the largest breakpoint.

Example:

<div class="row sg-grid-example">
  <div class="col-sm-6of12 col-md-3of12 col-lg-2of12 col-xl-1of12">
    content
  </div>
</div>

This is an extreme example, and in most cases each column will have one or two classes applied. If you want a column to always be 3 of 12 (even at the smallest viewport) you could simply apply .col-3of12 and it will always take up the width of 3 of 12 columns.
